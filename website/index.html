Addressing Feedback

The feedback was overall positive but there were two main concerns expressed: How the stablization of the flosser worked and the product not being too distinguishable from existing products. To address the first point, the explanation of the stabilization feature was not included. We had some idea on how it would work, but wanted to keep it vague for future work (ensuring high quality). In regards to the product not being too distinguishable, its not meant to be. The point of the product is to still be a water flosser with some small additional features to help more niche audiences. The stablization feature, bite activation, pressure adjustment, and the charging station are just a re-design of the oroginal features but made more accessible.

Future Work

Conclusion

The Watr flosser 2.0 aims to revolutionize dental care, particularly for those with disabilities, by offering innovative features such as stabilization mechanisms. While addressing feedback, we acknowledge the need for clarity regarding this technology, ensuring ongoing refinement for optimal usability. Despite similarities to existing products, our focus remains on enhancing accessibility and functionality for more niche audiences.

Future Work

For future work, the most important thing to create is the explanation of the stabilization feature. Given the technological advancements in image stabilization used in smartphone cameras, a similar concept could be applied to WatrFlossr 2.0. By integrating gyro sensors into the flosser's design, it could detect and analyze involuntary hand movements characteristic of Parkinson's. Subsequently, micro-actuators within the flosser could adjust the water flow or nozzle position in real-time to compensate for these movements, ensuring precise and effective cleaning while minimizing potential discomfort or difficulty for users with Parkinson's
